# AVERA Market Positioning

**Date:** 21 April 2026  
**Status:** Draft

## Category

AVERA should define itself as:

`Automotive engineering evidence and change impact platform`

Alternative category language:

- automotive verification evidence layer
- engineering memory platform for software-defined vehicles
- risk and traceability layer for vehicle engineering
- proof-backed change impact system for automotive teams

## One-Line Positioning

AVERA proves whether an automotive engineering change introduced a measurable verification or safety regression, and shows the evidence chain behind that conclusion.

## Short Positioning

AVERA is an automotive engineering evidence platform that connects changes, requirements, tests, simulations, logs, and risk into a proof-backed report. It helps engineering teams understand what changed, what can break, what evidence exists, and what must be verified before release.

## What AVERA Is

AVERA is:

- an evidence layer above existing engineering tools
- a change impact analyzer
- a verification evidence system
- a risk and confidence classifier
- a long-term engineering memory direction
- a standalone automotive engineering evidence product

## What AVERA Is Not

AVERA is not:

- a CAD replacement
- a PLM replacement
- a generic chatbot
- a full simulation solver
- an automatic certification authority
- a universal autonomous driving platform

## Initial Wedge

Start with:

`Automotive software verification evidence`

Best first domains:

- BMS software
- ECU control logic
- ADAS validation scenarios
- SDV integration testing

Best first workflow:

```text
engineering change description + requirements + baseline/current results -> evidence-backed risk report
```

## Target Users

First users:

- embedded automotive software engineers
- validation engineers
- BMS and ECU teams
- ADAS software teams
- software-defined vehicle platform teams

Later users:

- systems engineers
- safety engineers
- compliance teams
- supplier quality engineers
- manufacturing quality teams
- warranty and reliability analysts

## Buyer Pain

The pain is not lack of another AI assistant.

The pain is that engineering evidence is fragmented across:

- code repositories
- requirements tools
- test logs
- local and lab test systems
- simulation outputs
- SIL/HIL reports
- spreadsheets
- tickets
- supplier packages
- service and warranty systems

AVERA should turn this scattered evidence into a connected proof chain.

## Main Buyer Question

The product should answer:

> Can we prove what this change affects and whether it is safe enough to move forward?

## Competitive Frame

Large engineering platforms may offer AI inside CAD, CAE, PLM, simulation, or automation tools.

AVERA should not compete head-on with those platforms.

Instead, AVERA should sit across them as the proof and traceability layer:

```text
tool outputs -> normalized evidence -> risk reasoning -> release report
```

## Durable 20-30 Year Bet

Vehicle technology will change. File formats, tools, and architectures will change.

The durable need is:

- traceability
- proof
- evidence preservation
- risk reasoning
- engineering memory
- lifecycle accountability

That is the long-term market position for AVERA.
